Sources

Every feature in the dashboard traces back to a public, citation-grade dataset. The training pipeline (training/00–13) loads each source into a DuckDB panel; full ETL is in the project repo.

Regime & political indicators V-Dem v16

Varieties of Democracy dataset, country-year coverage 1789–2024. Used for civil_liberties (v2x_civlib), loser_consent (v2elaccept_ord), political_violence_ord_lag1 (v2caviol_ord), and mass_mobilization_ord_lag1 (v2cagenmob_ord). Loaded via the vdemdata R package.

Citation: Coppedge et al. (2025), V-Dem Codebook v16, v-dem.net.

Economic indicators WDI + IMF WEO

World Bank World Development Indicators for log_gdp_per_cap, gdp_growth, cpi_inflation, and unemployment. IMF World Economic Outlook backfills forward years and supplies projections for 2025+. State-managed economies (Cuba, PRK, Belarus, Venezuela, Eritrea, Turkmenistan, Cambodia, Laos) use hand-curated IMF Article IV estimates that include informal-sector and underemployment.

CPI is clipped to [−10, 200]% so hyperinflation outliers (Venezuela 2017, Zimbabwe 2008) don't dominate the fit. Country-years with no real CPI source are imputed from the regional median and flagged with cpi_imputed = 1 — those rows are dropped from training but preserved in the dashboard as a data-quality indicator.

Conflict events UCDP GED

Uppsala Conflict Data Program Georeferenced Event Dataset for the ≥100-fatality state-based-conflict threshold that defines the violence tier. Also used to derive conflict_neighbors_count (UCDP events ≥100 fatalities in same-region countries, lagged one year).

Coup attempts Cline Center

Cline Center Coup d'État Project for coup-attempt events used in the armed-violence outcome label.

Mass protests MMP

Mass Mobilization Project (Clark & Regan) for country-defining protest waves of ≥0.1% of population, used in the mobilization-tier outcome label alongside V-Dem's mass-mobilization ordinal.

Refugee flows UNHCR

UNHCR Refugee Population Statistics. log_refugee_outflow is log1p of net refugee outflow = (refugees + asylum-seekers originating here) − (refugees + asylum-seekers hosted here). Log-transformed so Afghanistan/Syria (millions) don't dominate the model relative to countries with thousands.

Coup-proofing fragmentation Pilster-Böhmelt

Pilster & Böhmelt's "effective number" of separate military forces (parallel guards, presidential security, etc.). Higher = regime fragmented its security apparatus to coup-proof. Counterintuitively, more fragmented = more unrest in the data.

Diagnostic-only sources loaded, unfit

The training panel also loads de Bruin (counterbalancing / politicization), Fariss (latent human-rights), Polity2, and FAO FFPI (global food-price index). These are kept for analysis but excluded from the fit: Fariss/Polity2 correlate ~0.9 with civil_liberties, de Bruin ends 2010, and FFPI is identical across countries within a year (no cross-sectional variation).

External benchmark ViEWS

ViEWS armed-conflict forecasts are loaded as a dashboard comparison row to ground-truth the model's violence-tier predictions against a well-established academic forecasting system.
