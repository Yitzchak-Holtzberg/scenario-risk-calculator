Methodology

A two-tier logistic regression with monotonicity constraints on every coefficient, fit on country-year panel data 1995–2022. The dashboard forecasts unrest in the year after the most recent observed snapshot.

Outcome: two tiers, fit separately

The model predicts two distinct channels of unrest, each with its own pooled LASSO plus four regime-stratified LASSOs:

Mass mobilization — a country-defining protest wave in the next 12 months. Labelled positive when (a) MMP records a large protest of ≥0.1% of population, or (b) V-Dem's v2cagenmob_ord rates the year ≥4. Routine protest frequency does not count.

Armed violence / coup — a regime-threatening armed event in the next 12 months. Labelled positive when (a) UCDP records ≥100 fatalities in state-based armed conflict, or (b) the Cline coup database records a coup attempt.

Both tiers share the same 10 features. The interesting empirical question is whether economic features matter more for armed violence than for mobilization — they should, based on civil-war literature.

Features

All 10 features are raw cross-country levels, no within-country z-scoring. civil_liberties is the regime-quality channel; regime-type dummies were dropped from the fit because they're collinear with it.

Monotonicity constraints

Every coefficient is forced to the sign its substantive interpretation requires. Civil liberties cannot increase modeled unrest; prior-year violence cannot decrease it. Constraints are enforced through glmnet's lower/upper bound arguments at fit time, not post-hoc — so the LASSO penalty operates in the constrained subspace. This trades a small amount of in-sample fit for guaranteed-coherent counterfactuals when an analyst drags a slider on the dashboard.

Lag structure

Both political_violence_ord_lag1 and mass_mobilization_ord_lag1 are lagged by one year so they don't overlap with the outcome label (which itself includes the current-year V-Dem ordinal). Without the lag the model would trivially "predict" violence by reading violence. conflict_neighbors_count is also lagged because UCDP releases late and same-year neighbor conflict isn't observable in real forecasting.

Training filter

Listwise-drop rows where cpi_imputed = 1 — country-years with no real CPI source where inflation was region-median imputed. Avoids fitting on synthetic inflation for PRK, pre-2018 Cuba, Somalia, etc. The cpi_imputed flag is preserved as a data-quality indicator in the dashboard, not as a model feature.

Train / holdout split

Train on country-years 1995–2022; predict 2023 unrest as the holdout check. The dashboard's "current snapshot" prediction is for the next 12 months from the most recent year of observed feature data.

Coefficient persistence & uncertainty

Fitted coefficients are persisted with bootstrap-derived empirical covariance so the dashboard can render 90% credible intervals alongside point predictions. The slider-driven scenario UI reads the stored coefficients directly — no model re-fit happens client-side.

Versions & provenance

The dashboard footer shows the model artifact key (e.g. unrest-risk-v0.8-mob-lag) and the date it was fitted. Each model version is a frozen export from the training pipeline at training/12_export_artifacts.qmd; the version key in the footer is the load-bearing identifier when comparing forecasts across iterations.

Diagnostic features kept but unfit

Several sources are loaded into the training panel for analysis but excluded from the fit: Fariss latent human-rights and Polity2 (both ~0.9 correlated with civil_liberties), de Bruin counterbalancing / politicization (source ends 2010), and FAO FFPI (global food-price index — identical across countries within a year, no cross-sectional variation to exploit).
